### <u> Law of Large Numbers Visualizer </u>

Two key results of probability theory are the Weak and Strong Laws of Large Numbers. In essence, they say a large number of random variables can (probably) have an *average* close to the *mean* of the distribution.

<a href="https://garrett-kepler.github.io/resources/LLN/"> Click here for an interactive visualization </a>

### <u>So... You Want to be a Gerrymanderer?</u>

The idea of cleverly picking groups to advantage your own interests sounds like something straight out of a board game. To understand gerrymandering, it might be best to try it for yourself!

## Resources:

Below are some open-source data sources that I have used personally and find to be easy to use.

- "*Redistricting Data Hub*" (<a href="https://redistrictingdatahub.org/">https://redistrictingdatahub.org/</a>) -- Collection of datasets for every US state at mostly every level of granularity necessary.
- "*Data.gov*" (<a href="https://data.gov/">https://data.gov/</a>) -- A home for all the open data available from the US government
- "*Network Data Repository*" (<a href="https://networkrepository.com/">https://networkrepository.com/</a>) -- A hub with specifically network-based datasets of all sorts (e.g. biological, social, transportation, etc.)
